# 304 RULES

## INTRODUCTION
    304 is a card gamer popular in Sri Lanka.  It is a four-player partnership point-trick game with bidding. Total points in the pack total 304, hence the name. This set of rules describes the variant me and my friends play, with certain pre-agreed house rules in effect.

    Adapted from: https://www.pagat.com/jass/304.html

## PLAYERS AND CARDS

- There are 4 players, in teams of two
- Each team sits opposing each other
- Deal and play is done anti-clockwise
- 304 uses a standard 32-card deck consistiung of the cards 7,8,9,10,J,Q,K,A in the 4 suits (8*4 = 32)
- The card game point values, and the rank of the cards from high to low in all suits is as follows:

|   Face value  |   Point value |
| ------------- | -------------- |
|   J   |   30  |
|   9   |   20  |
|   A   |   11  |
|   10  |   10  |
|   K   |   3   |
|   Q   |   2   |
|   8   |   0   |
|   7   |   0   |

- Note, this is the precise face value order, where J has precedence over 9, and so forth. 8 beats 7 (even though both are 0 value cards).

## SETUP

To begin with, the deck is sorted into the pack and stone, and is set up accordingly. Deck may be thoroughly shuffled. Any player may begin to deal first. 

## FLOW OF DEAL

- The dealer shuffles the cards, this must consist of (literally) 1-4 overhand shuffles
- NOTE: Minimal shuffling is key for preserving the order of cards to encourage high-value betting
- After the dealer has shuffled the cards, the player on the dealer's left is given the opportunity to cut the cards. They may choose not to, in which they tap the top of the deck to signify this. 
- The dealer then deals a batch of 4 cards to each player, beginning to the dealer's right, going round anti-clockwise and ending with the dealer
- The players look at the first 4 cards and bid according to what points they can win in rounds
- After the bidding is complete, the highest bidder puts down a trump card, and the dealer deals the remaining cards, again in batches of four, so that everyone has eight cards. A second round of bidding is done (on a must higher threshold).
- After each hand, the turn to deal passes anti-clockwise. 

## WITHIN A DEAL

### BIDDING AND CHOOSING TRUMP

#### BIDDING ON 4 CARDS

- Once 4 cards are dealt, the players bid in turn beginning from the right of the dealer (and anti-clockwise)
- Bidding begins at a minimum of 160, and each player must bid in increments of 10 (until above 100, where the increments are of 5)
- A player may choose to bid, pass or partner
- Bid - a player chooses a bid larger than the minimum bid in the game (with sufficient increment)
- Pass - a player chooses to not bid
- Partner - a player allows their partner to bid before thir opponents 
- NOTE: Bidding shorthand involves dropping 100 from the bid. Thus "60" is an informal (but is commonly practiced) as the bet name for 160. So forth for "100" -> 200.
- Bidding is complete after 3 consecutive passes.

##### Constraints on bidding below 200

- This can only be done on the first 4 cards.
- You can only bid below 200 given that you haven't passed or partnered (i.e. first time to speak), and that the minimum bets haven't surpassed 200
- If your partner has the highest when it is your turn to bid, your minimum bet is 200 (to take over from your partner)
- If you have previously already bet in the betting phase, then your minimum possible bet is 200 (or the highest bet, whichever is higher)

##### Reshuffling on 4 cards

Given the following constraints, you may trigger a reshuffle on 4 cards, causing a replay of the round (i.e. same dealer).
- If you are the first to bet
- If your partner has "partnered" and they were first to bet
- You have less than 15 points in total in your first 4 cards (see PLAYERS AND CARDS to add up face values)

NOTE: This is optional, a player with less than 15 points may still choose to bet, though unwise.

##### Choosing the Trump Suit

- The highest bidder from the BIDDING ON 4 CARDS Phase selects one of their cards and places it face down, without showing it to thte other players
- This is called the "Trump"
- The suit of this card is the trump suit

#### BIDDING ON 8 CARDS

- After the highest bidder on 4 cards is established, the dealer completes the deal with another batch of 4 cards to each player. 
- When everyone has looked at their 8 cards, their is a second round of bidding
- The bidding begins from the right of the dealer (like betting on 4 cards)
- In 8 card betting, bids begin at a minimum of 220, and minimum increment by 5.
- If anyone does make an 8-card bet, this supercedes the four-card bid. The 4 card bidder takes back their trump card and puts it back into their hand, and the 8-card bidder becomes the trump maker. 
- NOTE: Common slang for a 220 bet is "Honest". Any increments above 220, e.g. 225 is termed "Honest 5" and so forth. 
- NOTE 2: Once a player has bet, passed or partnered on the 8-card betting round, their minimum next bid is 250 
- NOTE 3: There is no slang for 250, termed only as 250.
- NOTE 4: If a player seeks to overbet a player on 250, their only option is PCC/"Partner Closed Caps", and this is an undertaking to win all 8 rounds 
- NOTE 5: The original bidder from 4-card betting can overbet themselves, and they may change their trump suit if needed.

### Play

- The player to the dealer's right leads the first round
- The winner of each round leads to the next
- The leader plays a card face up, and the others in turn each play a card
- They must follow suit, playing a card of the same suit that was led, if they can.
- If they are unable to follow suit, they may play any card.
- The trick is won by the highest trump in it, or, if it contains no trump, by the highest card of the suit that was led. 
- The cards of the trick are gathered and stored face down in a round pile of the team that won the trick
- NOTE: A team can only see the face up cards of the previous round that went if the next round has not proceeded. Past rounds may not be viewed
